Albert Einstein

Theoretical physicist • Nobel laureate • Father of modern physics

Biography

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist, widely acknowledged as one of the greatest and most influential physicists of all time. He is best known for developing the theory of relativity and the famous equation E = mc².

Major Contributions

  • Theory of Special Relativity (1905)
  • General Theory of Relativity (1915)
  • Photoelectric Effect (Nobel Prize, 1921)
  • Brownian Motion and foundational work in quantum theory
